Introduction

What's Swipe Out Hunger?

Swipe Out Hunger is the leading nonprofit addressing hunger amongst college students. Founded by a group of friends at UCLA in 2010, we have grown campus by campus into a national movement serving 1.5 million nutritious meals to date.

Looking ahead, we have refreshed our visual identity and core messaging to better represent the scope of our work and strengthen our appeal to the partners, school administrators and other audiences that can help us end student hunger.

The following outlines guidelines for a standardized Swipe Out Hunger brand – designed to ensure that all communications strengthen and extend our mission. We hope these resources will help us elevate our message and unify all aspects of our work.

If you have questions about these guidelines, run into trouble deciding which logo to use or need help with branding or design for an upcoming opportunity, please just email us.

Tagline

Our tagline is a summary statement of our mission and promise to the people we serve:

The tagline should emerge in all of our branded materials and set the tone for how we present Swipe Out Hunger’s success stories and opportunities.

When using the tagline in writing, it should be highlighted in italics. If set off from inline text, the tagline should always appear on two lines (with a break after “real”) and left-aligned.

Visual Identity

Logo

Our logo is the core of the Swipe Out Hunger style and identity. Its forward slant symbolizes the forward movement of our organization and alludes to swiping a card.

The primary logo should only appear on white, green or light-colored solid backgrounds and photos. Avoid placing it on any other colors or patterns.

Incorrect Logo Uses

Don't:
✕ Change logo colors
✕ Skew logo proportions
✕ Add effects or new elements to the logo
✕ Isolate parts of the logo
✕ Place type or other graphic elements too close to the logo
✕ Place our logo on cluttered backgrounds

Typography

Barlow Condensed Bold is the typeface that makes up the Swipe Out Hunger logos and core identity.

Work Sans is a secondary font that should only be used as a complement to the primary type as body text.


The following samples, with copy-friendly CSS for web uses, represent a range of brand-friendly typography styles. Please refer to these specs or use pre-styled templates when preparing branded communications.

Highlight Style

Our "highlight" treatment, based on the shapes in our logo can draw attention to headlines or key points and reinforce our brand. In all uses, the highlight shape should be proportionally scaled and/or extended using a vector design program to work with the typography. Angles on the left and right ends of the shape should always be 83º.
